Introduction & Motivation

Why Ethical AI?

  • AI systems increasingly make critical decisions affecting human lives
  • Need for transparent, justified ethical reasoning in machines
  • Traditional rule-based systems fail to capture ethical nuances
  • "Black box" problem: lack of explainability in AI decisions

The Challenge

How can we create computational systems that reason about ethics in a transparent, philosophically grounded manner?

Our Solution

  • Formalize multiple ethical frameworks in logical structure
  • Provide explicit reasoning traces for all decisions
  • Enable comparison across different moral perspectives
  • Create an educational tool for understanding ethics

Theoretical Framework (1/2)

1. Utilitarianism

Principle: Maximize overall happiness and minimize suffering

Utility(A) = Œ£ (Probability(Ci) √ó Value(Ci) √ó PeopleAffected(Ci))

Focus: Consequences and outcomes for the greatest number

2. Deontology

Principle: Actions must follow moral duties and rules

  • Respect autonomy and human dignity
  • Treat people as ends, never merely as means
  • Follow universal moral laws (Categorical Imperative)
  • Duty-based, regardless of consequences

Reasoning Algorithms

Main Reasoning Flow

1. User Query: recommend_action(DilemmaID, Framework, ?Action, ?Score)
2. Retrieve possible actions for dilemma
3. For each action: evaluate_action(DilemmaId, Framework, Action, Score)
4. Dispatch to framework-specific evaluator
5. Apply framework reasoning rules
6. Calculate numerical score
7. Return action with highest score
